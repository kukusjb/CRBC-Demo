Common questions

What first-time visitors usually ask

If you've never been to Canopy Roads — or haven't been to church in a long time — it's normal to have questions. Here are the most common ones we hear, answered honestly.

What should I wear?

Come as you are — literally. You'll see everything from jeans and t-shirts to Sunday dresses. We don't have a dress code, and we mean that. The goal is for you to feel comfortable, not put together.

Where do I park?

There's free parking in our main lot off Bannerman Road. If the main lot is full, overflow parking is available nearby and we'll have folks directing you. Plan to arrive 10 minutes early on your first visit.

Where do I go when I arrive?

Head to the main entrance — you'll be greeted by someone from our Welcome Team. Let them know it's your first time and they'll help you find your footing, get your kids checked in, and grab coffee before the service starts.

Will I be put on the spot?

No. You won't be asked to stand up, introduce yourself, or do anything uncomfortable. You're welcome to simply show up, observe, and decide at your own pace whether this is a place for you.

Do I need to give money?

Not at all. Giving is part of our worship as members, but as a first-time guest you're our guest — full stop. Don't let anything about giving be a reason not to come.

What's the service like?

Services run about 75 minutes. We open with live worship music — contemporary and singable — followed by a sermon from the Bible that's practical and direct. It's the same message at both services.

I haven't been to church in years. Is that okay?

More than okay. A lot of people at Canopy Roads came back to faith after a long time away, or came for the first time as adults. You don't need a church background to belong here.

What about accessibility?

Our building is fully wheelchair accessible with reserved parking close to the main entrance. Hearing-assistance devices are available at the welcome desk. Please reach out ahead of time if you have any specific needs.

Your first Sunday

What happens when you arrive

1

Pull into the parking lot

Arrive 10–15 minutes early. Our parking lot off Bannerman Rd is free and usually well-staffed on Sunday mornings to direct you to a spot.

2

Walk through the front doors

You'll be greeted by name (or a warm smile if it's your first time). Tell someone it's your first visit — they'll make sure you're taken care of.

3

Check in your kids (if you have them)

Head to the check-in kiosks in the kids' hallway. It takes about 5 minutes the first time. Your child will be in a safe, age-appropriate environment the whole service.

4

Grab a coffee and find a seat

We usually have coffee available in the lobby. Sit wherever feels right — there are no reserved seats. The worship team will start right on time.

5

Just be present

No agenda for your first visit. Worship with us if you'd like, or just listen. A service runs about 75 minutes — music, then a message from the Bible.

6

Meet someone (no pressure)

After the service, feel free to linger and introduce yourself — or slip out quietly. Both are totally fine. We'd love to connect, but we'll never chase you down.

Kids & students

Your kids are welcome and expected

We love kids at Canopy Roads — not just in theory, but in practice. We've built dedicated, fully-staffed spaces for every age group so your whole family can engage on Sunday morning.

Nursery & Preschool — Birth through Pre-K, both services
Elementary (CRBC Kids) — Kindergarten through 5th grade, both services
Students — Middle & high school, check the calendar for times

All volunteers are background-checked. Our check-in system gives you a security tag matched to your child — they're only released to you.
